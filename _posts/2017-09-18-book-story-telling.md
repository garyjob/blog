 	- A springboard story can be a powerful tool used to catalyze organization wide behavioral change
 	- A springboard story is around 55 words long
 	- there must be a tale about a protagonist instead of a large group
 	- there must be a challenge the protagonist is trying to surmount
 	- there must be enough details to convey the emotions the protagonist experience
 	- Leave room in the story so that audience could fill up the gap with their own context and imagine themselves to be the protagonist
 	- examples:

 	The bible - a collection of stories
 	- Miyamoto Musashi
 	- the Good Samaritan
 	- JFDI's method to pitch Seed stage startups

 	- To use method, need to collect stories that audience could relate to so as to elicit emotion
 	- Chief challenge of an anthropologist is to gain acceptance into the tribe she is trying to study / find story
 	- CEOs, shamans and village chiefs use this tool to weave the narrative and thus influence the culture of their tribes
 	- It is the intangible side of the tasks and process which are the tangible side of things - Perseus versus Medusa
