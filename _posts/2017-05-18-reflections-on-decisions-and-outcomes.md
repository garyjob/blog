 	- Making a good decision does not necessary lead to good outcomes
 	- Importance of a well refined mission statement

 	Helps deal with the Fog of War problem

 	Helps prevents analysis paralysis
 	- Reduces the likelihood of regret when viewing the decision in hindsight - since the same exact choice would have been made given more clarity in a future time period

 	- Increase speed of decision making:

 	allows a person to quickly determine the option most aligned with the mission statement

 	- Increase quality of decision making:

 	Increases the likelihood that the decision will stick after its been made
 	- Ensures resources are deployed to maximally impact intended outcome as defined in mission statement
